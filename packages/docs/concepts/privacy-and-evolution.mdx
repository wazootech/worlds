# Privacy and Evolution

Worlds is more than a technical framework; it is a commitment to the
**Local-First Manifesto**. We believe that as AI agents become more autonomous,
the knowledge they carry must remain the property of the humans they serve.

## The Local-First Manifesto

We strictly adhere to three core principles for agent memory:

- **Residency**: Your data should live as close to you as possible (the Edge).
- **Ownership**: You own the triples; Worlds is just the facilitator.
- **Portability**: Your knowledge graph is an open standard (RDF), not a
  proprietary silo.

## Data Residency & Ethics

Depending on your deployment strategy, Worlds ensures that your data residency
requirements are met with surgical precision:

### 1. Zero-Cloud Development

When running locally, Worlds acts as an air-gapped memory pool. **No data ever
leaves your machine**. Embeddings are generated locally, and state is stored in
your private local filesystem.

### 2. Managed Orchestration (The Console)

In our managed platform, we enforce **strict tenant isolation**. Each World
occupies a dedicated, isolated compute worker. We never train public models on
your private symbolic state.

### 3. Enterprise Compliance

Because Worlds can run entirely offline or in per-region clusters, it simplifies
compliance for **GDPR**, **HIPAA**, and **SOC 2**. You control the network
perimeter; Worlds provides the memory.

---

## Evolution Roadmap

Worlds is an evolving platform. Our goal is to expand the "Symbiotic Base Layer"
through the following initiatives:

- **Automated Resonance**: Advanced "Folder Watchers" that turn local directory
  changes into instant symbolic state.
- **Ecosystem Tooling**: VS Code extensions for SPARQL debugging and a native
  Python SDK for data science integration.
- **Inference Rules**: Moving from "Fact Storage" to "Reasoning Engines" that
  automatically infer new relationships based on logical patterns.
