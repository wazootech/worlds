# Ontology & Schema

If **Entities** are the atoms of your agent's memory, the **Ontology** is the
grammar that dictates how they interact.

In a Neuro-Symbolic system, the ontology acts as the formal bridge between
**unstructured data** and **structured symbolic logic**. It defines what types
of things exist in your World and what they are allowed to do.

## Why Structure Matters

Without an ontology, an agent's memory is a chaotic pile of text chunks. With an
ontology, the agent understands the **nature** of the information it is
processing.

- **Predictable Relationships**: Instead of guessing if a user "likes" or
  "loves" a project, the ontology defines a single, stable predicate:
  `w:prefers`.
- **Type Safety for AI**: When the agent extracts a new fact, it checks the
  ontology to ensure it is assigning properties to the right type of entity
  (e.g., a `Task` has a `deadline`, but a `User` has a `role`).
- **Interoperability**: By using standardized schemas (like Schema.org or custom
  Wazoo namespaces), you ensure that your agent's memory can share knowledge
  with other agents and tools seamlessly.

## Core Components

### 1. Classes (The "What")

Classes define the categories of entities in your World.

- **Examples:** `User`, `Project`, `SoftwareModule`, `DesignAsset`.
- **Function:** Tells the engine, "This node is a specific _kind_ of thing."

### 2. Predicates (The "How")

Predicates (or Properties) define the relationships between entities or between
an entity and a value.

- **Examples:** `worksOn`, `assignedTo`, `hasPriority`, `memberOf`.
- **Function:** The "verbs" that create the edges in your graph.

## Generative AI Fact Extraction

This is where formal structure meets unstructured input. When you ingest raw
text, the Worlds engine doesn't just look for keywords. It performs
**Ontological Extraction**:

1. **Generative AI Input**: An LLM reads the unstructured text: _"Ethan is
   leading the API redesign."_
2. **Symbolic Mapping**: The engine looks at your World's ontology and
   identifies that "Ethan" is a `User` and "API redesign" is a `Project`.
3. **Fact Creation**: It maps the relationship to the formal predicate
   `w:leadsProject`.

This mapping ensures that your "World" remains orderly and that your
**Verifiable Reasoning** queries remain accurate, providing a perfect **audit
trail** for every fact.

## Dynamic vs. Fixed Schemas

- **Fixed**: You can define a strict schema that your agent must follow,
  ensuring 100% data integrity for industrial applications.
- **Malleable**: Worlds can also "discover" new predicates as it learns,
  allowing the ontology to evolve as the agent encounters new domains of
  knowledge.
