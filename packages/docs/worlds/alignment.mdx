Worlds is the engine for personalized, alignment-driven software. By providing 
a stateful substrate for autonomous agents, it enables the practical 
application of Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) to steer 
high-dimensional model behavior.

## Probability reshaping

In its most raw form, RLHF is the process of sculpting a probability landscape. 
An LLM starts as a map of all possible word sequences. RLHF acts as a series 
of nudges in token space:

*   Logit shift: increases raw scores for preferred paths and suppresses undesired ones.
*   Softmax filter: ensures that during inference, the model is statistically driven toward helpfulness and safety.

### Reward hacking
Without grounding, a model suffers from **reward hacking**, where it finds 
cheats in the token space—like repetitive or sycophantic phrasing—to 
maximize reward without utility. 

Worlds provides the **verifiable context** required to monitor and mitigate 
these hacks by grounding model outputs in the immutable graph ledger.

## Recursive quality loop

The future of intentional agency relies on the recursive quality loop. 
This transforms human and autonomous feedback into stateful knowledge.

### Feedback as state

When a human or AI judge approves or corrects an agent's reasoning, that 
feedback is asserted as a verifiable triple in the world's ledger:

1.  **Capture preference**: The agent records a preference signal for a 
    specific query result or relationship.
2.  **Assert triple**: The system inserts mutation quads (e.g., 
    `<result_id> worlds:hasPreference worlds:Accepted`).
3.  **Refine state**: Future queries follow the reshaped probability 
    landscape toward high-reward paths.

## Scalable supervision

As systems scale, human evaluation becomes a bottleneck. Worlds enables 
**Scalable Supervision** (RLAIF) by using high-reasoning models to evaluate 
candidate triples against a world model.

### Continuous evaluation (Evals)

Scalable Supervision is the operationalized form of an **Eval**.
- **AI Judges**: Models evaluate candidate facts for consistency and safety.
- **Autonomous Rewards**: Feedback is asserted autonomously (e.g., 
  `result:123 worlds:hasReward 0.95`), allowing the system to scale its 
  alignment without manual intervention.

## Constitutional AI

Worlds provides the stateful substrate for **Constitutional AI (CAI)**—alignment 
guided by a core set of principles (the ontology) rather than manual labels.

*   **Self-Critique**: Agents use the graph to identify if their proposed 
    triples violate any constitutional rules.
*   **Collaborating Agents**: Independent validator agents cross-verify 
    knowledge. Every approval is a reified fact (`worlds:verifiedBy :agent_A`), 
    creating an auditable log of the alignment process.

## Autonomous discovery

By leveraging the graph as the feedback mechanism, agents can grow a dataset 
and ontology from scratch. This is known as Reinforcement Learning from 
Knowledge Graph Feedback (RLKGF).

### Growing from scratch

Agents "discover" the world through a recursive alignment loop:
1.  **Competency questions**: The agent analyzes raw input to generate queries 
    about gaps in the graph.
2.  **Self-correcting ontologies**: The agent proposes and tests new classes 
    for logical consistency using SPARQL ASK queries. 
3.  **Reward signal**: If the structure maintain logic and answers 
    the questions, it receives a higher reward.

This transforms the world model from a snapshot into an evolving, 
agent-driven memory that remains verifiable through the permanent ledger.

### Academic grounding

This recursive approach to alignment is supported by major AI research:

*   Recursive reward modeling (RRM): Jan Leike et al. (OpenAI) argue in 
    ["Scalable agent alignment via reward modeling"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1811.07871) 
    that breaking complex evaluations into recursive sub-tasks is key to 
    scaling oversight.
*   Constitutional AI (CAI): The framework pioneered by Anthropic for 
    aligning models via self-critique and principle-based "Constitutions." 
*   Recursive self-improvement (RSI): The theoretical basis for systems found 
    in the work of Bostrom and Yudkowsky, where meta-optimization of schemas 
    leads to optimal state configurations.
