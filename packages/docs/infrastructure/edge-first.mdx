# Edge-First Infrastructure

Worlds is designed with an **"Edge-First"** philosophy, part of our commitment
to the **Small Web**—a future where users maintain autonomy, control their own
data, and operate in an interoperable ecosystem.

## Building for the Small Web

We believe that the best place for an agent's memory is as close to the user as
possible. Whether it's on a local machine, a private server, or at the network
edge, Worlds ensures that intelligence and knowledge live where you do.

### 1. Local Autonomy

Your agents should never depend on a central cloud for their memory. Worlds
allows for localized deployment of the triplestore and API, ensuring that even
if the internet goes down, your agent's memory remains intact and functional.

### 2. Privacy & Data Ownership

By keeping the Knowledge Graph at the edge, sensitive relationship data and
personal facts never have to leave your controlled environment. We treat memory
as a private "Digital Garden" that you own and curate, rather than data points
in a centralized silo.

### 3. Low Latency Reasoning

Reasoning over a graph requires high-speed access to facts. Retrieving memory
from a local or regional edge node is significantly faster than querying a
centralized global database, enabling agents to respond in milliseconds.

## Deployment Models

- **Standard Cloud**: Deploying a Worlds node on Vercel or Railway for ease of
  use.
- **Local Native**: Running the Worlds CLI and server on your local
  machine—ideal for high-privacy "Digital Gardening."
- **Hardware Edge**: Specialized deployments for robotics or local hardware
  controllers where autonomy is critical.
