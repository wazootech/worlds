Worlds is built on a foundation of professional, neuro-symbolic engineering and
local-first principles. We believe that for AI agents to become truly autonomous
and reliable, they require a persistent memory infrastructure that is as
structured as it is accessible.

## Core pillars

We bridge the gap between AI intuition and deterministic ownership using several
fundamental pillars.

### Neuro-symbolic synthesis

Worlds uses a hybrid architecture to solve the reasoning gap in modern AI.

- **Neural (LLM intuition)**: Generative AI extracts meaning from unstructured
  data.
- **Symbolic (RDF logic)**: The queryable graph stores that meaning as
  deterministic facts. This ensures your agent navigates a structured map of
  reality rather than guessing context based on probabilities.

### Edge-first autonomy

An agent's memory must live close to the user. Worlds ensures that intelligence
and knowledge live on your local machine, a private server, or at the network
edge.

- **Local autonomy**: Agents never depend on a central cloud for their memory.
- **Low latency**: Local nodes retrieve memory significantly faster than global
  databases.

### The local-first manifesto

As agents become more autonomous, humans must retain ownership of the knowledge
those agents carry.

- **Residency**: Data lives close to you.
- **Ownership**: You own the triples; Worlds acts as the facilitator.
- **Portability**: The graph uses an open standard (RDF), preventing proprietary
  silos.

## Technical values

Beyond our core pillars, we adhere to several technical values that guide the
development of the World Engine.

### Bring your own brain (BYOB)

The Worlds infrastructure is agnostic to the intelligence layer. Whether you
utilize OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, or local models, the world acts as a
detachable hippocampus. You maintain the flexibility to swap model providers
without losing your agent's persistent state.

### Calm technology

We believe developer tooling must remain invisible. We target zero-config
experiences where the complexities of graph management, SPARQL optimization, and
vector synchronization are abstracted away. Use standard tools, and the engine
handles the heavy lifting.

### Small web and interoperability

We envision a future where users maintain autonomy and control their data. By
building on permissive open-source standards (Apache 2.0) and utilizing
protocols like MCP, we enable a decentralized ecosystem of interoperable
knowledge graphs.

### Malleable knowledge

Data is not static. Knowledge must be as fluid as the environments it describes.
The Worlds architecture supports forking, merging, and mutating world models in
real-time.

A World acts as a **Digital Garden** for your agents—a private, evolving context
where relationships and history are maintained with deterministic integrity.
