---
title: "Manifesto"
description: "The next default for human knowledge"
---

Human progress tracks how information is stored, shared, and processed. In every
era, a new medium is born and civilization surges forward. Our mediums evolved
from ochre paintings on stone to spreadsheets on silicon.

For the vast majority of the human era, the brain was our only hard drive. Early
societies used [myth and song](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oral_tradition) as
mnemonic devices to encode critical data across generations.

The Agricultural Revolution forced humanity to manage surpluses and trade,
catalyzing the invention of
[**Cuneiform**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuneiform). Later, the Phoenicians
introduced the
[**phonetic alphabet**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenician_alphabet),
contributing to the democratization of literacy.

Millennia later, the
[**printing press**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Printing_press) collapsed the
cost of replication. This shifted the information bottleneck and fueled the
[Renaissance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance) and the
[Scientific Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_Revolution).

By the 20th century, we reached the physical limits of paper. In 1945, the
concept of the [**Memex**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memex) emerged,
designed after how the human mind operates. It proposed that information systems
allow users to link facts together. This became the first conceptual model for a
[knowledge graph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_graph).

In 1968, [hypertext](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hypertext) demonstrated the
potential of linked data, a vision later realized in pioneering systems at
[**Xerox PARC**](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PARC_(company)>) and Apple's
[**HyperCard**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HyperCard). These systems proved
the power of linked data.

To share this vision with the world, the
[W3C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Wide_Web_Consortium) standardized
HTML, CSS, and XML for the web. In 1999, they gave us
[**RDF**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resource_Description_Framework),
creating the interoperable primitives of linked data.

With the rise of autonomous agents, the externalized hard drive has learned to
think. The _medium itself_ can read, synthesize, pattern-match, and generate
novel observations.

**Worlds** realizes the original vision of the Semantic Web converged with the
bleeding edge of AI. It provides the stateful substrate required to unhobble
autonomous, neuro-symbolic AI agents by fusing
[Large Language Models](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_language_model) with
the [Semantic Web](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semantic_Web).

We believe human potential with computing remains critically handicapped.
Software is due to eat the world all over again, and we are hungry to help build
it.
