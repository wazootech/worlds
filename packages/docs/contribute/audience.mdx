# Understand Your Audience

To write effective documentation, it's critical to identify exactly who you are
writing for. Each piece of content should be focused on a specific user persona
to avoid compromises that satisfy no one.

## Our Primary Personas

The Worlds ecosystem is designed for developers building the next generation of
AI applications. We focus on three primary audiences:

### 1. The Agent Architect (Primary)

**Goal:** Building complex, stateful agents that require long-term memory and
relational reasoning.

- **Needs:** Deep dives into the Agentic Loop, stateful memory management, and
  how to bridge neural intuition with symbolic precision.
- **Prior Knowledge:** Familiar with LLM orchestration (LangChain, OpenAI SDKs)
  but struggling with context limits and "short-term memory loss."

### 2. The Infrastructure Lead

**Goal:** Ensuring data residency, privacy, and low-latency performance for
AI-driven workflows.

- **Needs:** Architecture overviews, local-first philosophy, deployment guides
  (Deno, Turso), and security specifications.
- **Prior Knowledge:** Experienced in cloud infrastructure, databases, and
  DevOps. Focused on the "Edge-First" capabilities of the platform.

### 3. The AI Integrator

**Goal:** Quickly adding persistence and verifiable truth to a simple AI
features or products.

- **Needs:** Quickstarts, "Vibe Coding" patterns, cookbook recipes, and
  easy-to-use SDK references.
- **Prior Knowledge:** General web development; wants to get things running fast
  without necessarily becoming an expert in RDF or SPARQL.

---

## User Research is Key

While these personas guide our writing, the best insights come from talking
directly to users.

- **How do they describe Worlds?** Do they use words like "Memory SDK," "Graph
  Database," or something else?
- **What are the pain points?** What is explicitly missing or confusing in the
  current documentation?
- **What are their goals?** What is the one thing they want to accomplish today?

> [!TIP] Before writing or editing any page, ask: **"Who is the reader, what are
> they trying to accomplish, and what do they already know?"**
